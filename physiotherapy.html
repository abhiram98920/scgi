Why Bachelor of Physiotherapy?

BPT course is for those who have always aspired to work in the field of human services. They can also help you incorporate complementary and alternative medicine into your practice. A student can learn about human anatomy while working with their hands in physiotherapy.

In India, there is a high demand for physiotherapists, with various types of patients requiring their services, including those recovering from surgery and accidents, as well as those suffering from chronic conditions such as arthritis.

As people become more concerned about their health and fitness, the incidence of sports injuries is predicted to rise, resulting in a higher need for physiotherapy services. Students who graduate with a Bachelor of Physiotherapy can work as sports physiotherapists, physiotherapists, osteopaths, or consultants.

Eligibility

10+2 class or its equivalent passed with aggregate of 40% marks in Science which consist of subjects Physics, Chemistry and Biology conducted by the department of pre-university education in anywhere in India. English Mandatory.

Eligibility for Examination

Students should have 80% attendance each in theory and practical. The student should complete 11 months duration of that particular academic year before appearing for the examination.

The principal should certify that the candidate’s performance and conduct are satisfactory. If a student fails in one theory paper or practical examination, she/he may sit for a supplementary examination in the failed subjects or practical held subsequently.
